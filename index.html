FAQ's

How can we help you?

If you still have questions, send us message!

How is Simplete different from other health insurance plans?

Simplete is a new concept in healthcare delivery with the goal to offer complete coverage that is simple to navigate. It brings providers and health insurance to the table in a new way, all to benefit patients throughout their healthcare journey. Simplete plans are designed by providers with you and your employees in mind. The plans are priced to save employers money on premiums and when their employees stay in network they are rewarded by lower out of pocket costs and a simple process.

What is a Tiered Provider Network?

With a Tiered-Provider network, how much a member pays out-of-pocket for services depends on the provider they see. In the case of Simplete, there are three tiers—Memorial Tier 1, In-Network Tier 2, Out-of-network Tier 3. Members will pay the least out of pocket for Memorial Tier 1 providers. The Out-of-network Tier 3 is only available with the Simplete POS plans.

What is the difference between Simplete plans?

The HMO plans offer two levels of in-network coverage. While members must use an in-network provider, they have access to both Memorial Tier 1 and in-network Tier 2 providers. With lower premiums these plans offer the most value for employer groups. POS plans offer more flexibility. Members have access to Tier 1 and Tier 2 providers and pay less out of pocket when using these tiers. If members are willing to pay more out-of-pocket, they also have the opportunity to use out-of-network Tier 3 providers.

Will my employees have to change doctors?

In most cases, your employees will still have in-network access to their doctors. Memorial, Springfield Clinic and SIU primary care physicians are all in-network providers. If some of your employees do not have doctors within these groups, we offer POS plans that allow members to go out of network to see another physician. In the provider directory (link), Tier 1 and Tier 2 providers are identified with a badge.

If this insurance plan is through Memorial, does that mean members can’t ever go to another hospital?

The collaboration between the health system and insurance provider allows for lower premiums and less costly in-network coverage. Members will pay the least amount out-of-pocket by going to Memorial Health System when they receive care locally, but other hospitals are also included in the network. And don’t worry! If a member has an emergency while out of town, they will be covered at the in-network benefit level.

Why is Simplete not available in my area?

Simplete is a new, innovative approach to health insurance. Initially, it will only be offered in eight of Memorial Health System's core counties (Cass, Christian, Logan, Mason, Menard, Morgan, Sangamon, Scott). We hope to expand this innovative approach further in 2020.
